Instructions for Meditating on Mind is Emptiness

  • In this level, the practice instructions pertain to the second pointing out instruction that introduces the mind to be emptiness.
  • For this practice, rest naturally without trying to control experience in any way, yet without being lost in distraction. This practice has two main components: analytical meditation (step 1) and resting meditation (step 2)
  • The first step is to examine the mind's location and characteristics.
    • Begin by resting your mind as it is. Next, let your mind face outward. After a few seconds, turn the mind inward — mind looking at mind. Then ask the following questions: "Where is the mind? What does the mind look like? Does it have any color, shape, or form? Does the mind have a beginning or end? Is it inside or outside the body?" Ask each of these questions one by one, and take time to observe your experience, and see what you find. Do not think about these questions intellectually. Observe your mind directly. Alternate this inquiry with resting in awareness from time to time.
    • When you investigate the mind, you will see that you cannot find it. The mind is open and empty, just like space. It has no shape, no form, no color, no beginning, no end, no outside, and no inside. It is completely empty. Yet, something knows it is empty. That knowing is the clarity. Awareness is still present. Your mind is the union of emptiness and clarity; you cannot find it when you look for it, yet it is not nothing because you still know and experience things. When you experience this "unfindability" of the mind, let go and rest in awareness.
  • Step two is the practice of mind looking at mind. Begin by resting your mind as it is for a few moments. Next, let the mind face out. After a few seconds, turn the mind inward, and let the mind look at itself. You do not have to ask any questions here, but simply let the mind look at the mind, and then rest. There is nothing to see, nothing to pinpoint. It is all empty. While being empty, you can still see and hear. Also, you know it is empty. This is the clarity. The union of emptiness and clarity is right here. You do not need to investigate for this step, simply look within, and then rest.
  • Throughout this exploration, the main practice is still to rest in awareness. You can intersperse periods of resting in awareness with a brief inquiry. For instance, you might begin by resting in awareness and then gently examine the mind's shape or boundaries for a few moments, and then rest again in awareness. In this way, you can alternate brief, gentle periods of inquiry with periods of resting naturally in awareness.
  • End your nature of mind practice by resting in awareness.
