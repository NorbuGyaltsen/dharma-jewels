The Heart Sutra - English

༄༅། །རྒྱ་གར་སྐད་དུ། བྷ་ག་ཝ་ཏི་པྲ་ཛྙ་པ་ར་མི་ཏཱ་ཧྲི་ད་ཡ། །བོད་སྐད་དུ། །བཅོམ་ལྡན་འདས་མ་ཤེས་རབ་ཀྱི་ཕ་རོལ་ཏུ་ཕྱིན་པའི་སྙིང་པོ།The Heart Sutra: The Essence of the Awakened Mother, the Perfection of Wisdom

Tibetan Chant

Tibetan Phonetics

gyagar kedu bhaga wati prajna paramtia hridaaya
From India (Sanskrit:) Bhagavati Prajna Paramita Hridayam

böe ketu chomden dé ma sherab kyi paroltu chinpe nyingpo bampo chig go
(Tibetan:) I bow to Bhagavati (Buddha / the transcendent victor / Blessed One) who from the other side brought the essence (Verses / Sutras) of perfected wisdom

diké dag gi töpa du chigna
Thus, have I heard

chomdendé gyalpo'i khab ja gar püngpo'i rila
At one time, the Blessed One dwelt in Rajagriha at Vulture Peak Mountain

gelong gi gendün chenpo dang
Together with a great sangha of fully ordained monks

jangchub sempa'i gendun chenpo dang tab chigtu zhug té
And a great sangha of bodhisattvas

de'i tsé
At that time

chomdendé zambo nangwa zhé jawé chökyi nam drang kyi ting ngé dzin la nyompar zhüg so
The Blessed One rested in the samadhi of the enumerations of phenomena called “perception of the profound”

yang détsé jangchub sempa sempa chenpo pagpa chenrezig wangchug
And at the same time noble Avalokiteshvara, the bodhisattva mahasattva

sherab kyi paroltu chinpa zabmo'i chöpa nyila nampar ta shing
While observing the practice of the profound Perfection of Wisdom, saw in this way:

püngpo ngapo dédag la yang rangzhin gyi tongpar nampa ta-o
He saw the five Skandhas (aggregates / elements) to be empty of nature

dené sangyé kyi tü
Then, through the power of the Buddha

tsé dang denpa sharibu
Venerable Shariputra said to

jangchub sempa sempa chenpo pagpa chenrezig wangchug la diké ché méso
the noble Avalokiteshvara, the bodhisattva mahasattva:

rigkyi bu gang lala sherab kyi paroltu chinpa zambo'i chöpa chepar döpa dé jitar labparja
“How should a son or daughter of noble family who wishes to practice the profound Perfection of Wisdom train?”

deké ché mépa dang
In response

jangchub sempa sempa chenpo pagpa chenrezig wangchug
The noble Avalokiteshvara, the bodhisattva mahasattva

gi tsé dang denpa shara dati bula diké ché mé so
said to venerable Shariputra:

sharibu rigkyi bu'am rigkyi bumo gang lala sherab kyi paroltu
“O Shariputra, sons and daughters of noble family who wish to

chinpa zambo'i chöpa chépar döpa dé ditar nampar tawar ja té
practice the profound Perfection of Wisdom should see clearly in this way:

püngpo ngapo dédag yang rang zhin gyi tongpar nampar yang dagpar jésu ta-o
They should see clearly that the five Skandas are empty of nature

züg tongpa-o tongpa nyi züg so
Form is emptiness; emptiness is form

züg lé tongpa nyi zhen mayin tongpa nyi lé kyang züg zhen mayinno
Emptiness is none other than form and form is none other than emptiness

de zhendu tsorwa dang dushé dang dujé dang nampar shépa nam tongpa-o
Just so, feeling, discrimination, formation, and consciousness are empty

sharibu détar chötamché tongpa nyidé
Thus, Shariputra, all phenomena are emptiness

tsen nyi mépa ma kyepa ma gagpa drima mépa
They have no characteristics. They do not arise, nor do they cease. They are not impure

drima dang drelwa driwa mépa gangwa mépa-o
Nor are they pure. They are not diminished, nor are they filled

sharibu dé ta wéna tongpa nyila zügmé
O Shariputra, thus in emptiness there is no form

tsorwa mé dushé mé duché nam mé nampar shépa mé
No feeling, no discrimination, no formation, and no consciousness

mig mé nawa mé na mé
No eyes, no ears, no nose

ché mé lu mé yi mé
No tongue, no body, and no mind

züg mé dra mé dri mé
No form, no sound, no smell

ro mé reja mé chö mé-do
No taste, no object of touch, and no phenomena

mig gi kham mépa né yi kyi kham mé
No eye element to no mind element

yi kyi nampar shepé kham kyi bardu tang médo
Up to no mental consciousness element

ma rigpa mé ma rigpa zépa mépa mé gashi mé
No ignorance and no end of ignorance, up to no old age and death

gashi zépé bardu yang mé do
And no end of old age and death

dé zhiindu dug ngel wa dang kün jungwa dang gogpa dang
Just so, there is no suffering, no source, no cessation

lam mé teshé mé tobpa mé ma tobpa-ang mé do
No path; no wisdom, no attainment, and no nonattainment

sharibu, dé tawéna jangchub sempa nam tob'pa mépé chir
Therefore, Shariputra, since bodhisattvas have nothing to attain

sherab kyi paroltu chinpa la tenching néte
They abide by and dwell in the Perfection of Wisdom

semla dribpa mépé dragpa médé
Since their minds are unobscured, they have no fear

chinchi loglé shintu déné nya ngen lédé parchin-to
Fully transcending illusion, they reach complete nirvana

dusüm du nampar zhügpa'i sangyé tamché kyang
All the buddhas throughout the three times

sherab kyi paroltu chinpa la téné
By relying on the Perfection of Wisdom

lana mépa tang dagpar dzogpa'i jangchub tu ngonpar dzogpar sangyé-so
Fully awaken to unsurpassed, perfect, complete Enlightenment

dé tawéna sherab kyi paroltu chinpa'i ngag
Therefore, the mantra of the Perfection of Wisdom

rigpa chenpo'i ngag lana mepa'i ngag
The mantra of great insight, the unsurpassed mantra

mi nyampa dang nyampa'i ngag
The mantra equal to that which has no equal

dugnygal tamché rabtu zhiwar chépa'i ngag
The mantra that completely calms all suffering

mi dzünpé na denpar shépar ja té
Should be known to be true, for it is not false

sherab kyi paroltu chinpa'i ngag mé pa
The Perfection of Wisdom mantra is spoken as follows:

TEYATA OM GATÉ GATÉ PARAGATÉ PARASAMGATÉ BODHI SOHA
Gone, gone, gone all the way, everyone gone to the other shore, hail Enlightenment

sharibu jangchub sempa sempa chenpo
Shariputra, a bodhisattva mahasattva

detar sherab kyi paroltu chinpa zabmo la labpar ja-o
Should practice the profound Perfection of Wisdom in this way”

déné chomdendé tingédzin délé zhengté
The Blessed One then arose from his samadhi

jangchub sempa sempa chenpo pagpa chenrezig wangchug
And said to noble Avalokiteshvara, the bodhisattva mahasattva

la lekso zhéjawa ghiné lekso lekso rigkyi bu dé dezhinno rigkyi bu dé dézhin té
“Good, good, child of noble family; thus it is, child of noble family; thus it is

jitar chekyi tenpa zhintu sherab kyi paroltu chinpa zambo la chépar jaté
The profound Perfection of Wisdom should be practiced just as you have taught

dézhin shegpa nam kyang jésu yirang ngo
And all the tatagatas (buddhas) will rejoice”

chondendé kyi déké chéka tsélné
When the Blessed One said this

tsedang denpa shara dati budang jangchub sempa chenrezig wangchug dang
Venerable Shariputra and noble Avalokiteshvara, the bodhisattva mahasattva

tamché dang denpa'i khor dé dang
Along with the entire retinue and

lha dang mi dang lhamayin dang drizar chépé jigten yi-rang-té
The world with its gods, humans, asuras (demi-gods), and gandharvas (celestial spirits) rejoiced

chomdendé kyi süngpa la ngönpar tö do
And praised the words of the Blessed One

English Translation

Thus, have I heard: At one time, the Blessed One dwelt in Rajagriha at Vulture Peak Mountain, together with a great sangha of fully ordained monks and a great sangha of bodhisattvas. As the Blessed One rested in the samadhi of the enumerations of phenomena called “perception of the profound,” noble Avalokiteshvara, the bodhisattva mahasattva, observed the practice of the profound Perfection of Wisdom and saw the five aggregates to be empty of nature.

Then, through the power of the Buddha, venerable Shariputra addressed noble Avalokiteshvara, the bodhisattva mahasattva: “How should a son or daughter of noble family who wishes to practice the profound Perfection of Wisdom train?”

In response, noble Avalokiteshvara, the bodhisattva mahasattva, said to venerable Shariputra, “O Shariputra, sons and daughters of noble family who wish to practice the profound Perfection of Wisdom should see clearly in this way. They should see clearly that the five aggregates are empty of nature.”

“Form is emptiness; emptiness is form. Emptiness is none other than form and form is none other than emptiness. Just so, feeling, discrimination, formation, and consciousness are empty. Thus, Shariputra, all phenomena are emptiness. They have no characteristics. They do not arise, nor do they cease. They are not impure, nor are they pure. They are not diminished, nor are they filled.”

“O Shariputra, thus in emptiness there is no form, no feeling, no discrimination, no formation, and no consciousness; no eyes, no ears, no nose, no tongue, no body, and no mind; no form, no sound, no smell, no taste, no object of touch, and no phenomena; no eye element to no mind element, up to no mental consciousness element; no ignorance and no end of ignorance, up to no old age and death and no end of old age and death. Just so, there is no suffering, no source, no cessation, and no path; no wisdom, no attainment, and no nonattainment.”

“Therefore, Shariputra, since bodhisattvas have nothing to attain, they abide by and dwell in the Perfection of Wisdom. Since their minds are unobscured, they have no fear. Completely transcending misconception, they reach complete nirvana. All the buddhas throughout the three times fully awaken to unsurpassed, perfect, complete enlightenment by relying on the Perfection of Wisdom.”

“Therefore, the mantra of the Perfection of Wisdom, the mantra of great insight, the unsurpassed mantra, the mantra equal to that which has no equal, the mantra that completely calms all suffering should be known to be true, for it is not false. The Perfection of Wisdom mantra is spoken as follows:

TEYATA OM GATÉ GATÉ PARAGATÉ PARASAMGATÉ BODHI SOHA
Gone, gone, gone all the way, everyone gone to the other shore, hail Enlightenment

“Shariputra, a bodhisattva mahasattva should practice the profound Perfection of Wisdom in this way.”

The Blessed One then arose from his samadhi and said to noble Avalokiteshvara, the bodhisattva mahasattva: “Good, good, child of noble family. Thus it is, child of noble family. Thus it is. The profound Perfection of Wisdom should be practiced just as you have taught and all the tatagatas will rejoice.” When the Blessed One said this, venerable Shariputra and noble Avalokiteshvara, the bodhisattva mahasattva, along with the entire retinue and the world with its gods, humans, demi-gods, and gandharvas rejoiced and praised the words of the Blessed One.

This concludes the noble Essence of the Perfection of Wisdom.
