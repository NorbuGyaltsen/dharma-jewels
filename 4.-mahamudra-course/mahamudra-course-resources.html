Mahamudra Course Resources

How to Use the Mahamudra Course

Core Teachings (30 to 60 minutes per week) If you have limited time or would like to stay focused on the essence of the Mahamudra path, we recommend you focus on the “Core Teachings” in each section. We have intentionally kept these selections brief so that even those with a very busy schedule can keep up.

Going Deeper (1 to 3 hours per week) If you have more time and are interested in exploring the Mahamudra path in more depth, we recommend that you start with the “Core Teachings” and then pick one or more of the other course elements to go deeper. You can add the traditional and contemporary readings, or pick one of the view, meditation, or application sections from the workbook and complete the practices and exercises.

The Full Experience (3 to 5 hours per week) If you have the time and are inspired to work with all of the readings, workbook exercises, and recommended practices, it will likely take you 3-4 hours per week for the study element, plus any additional time you spend working with the recommended practices and/or small group meetings.

Workbook and Supplementary Texts

  • Mahamudra Workbook The course comprises five main topics, or modules. This workbook is your main guide for navigating the modules and sections, and will also serve as a tool for reviewing the material throughout the course. This updated version of the workbook includes page numbers for readings from the new translation Moonbeams of Mahamudra translated by Elizabeth Callahan (see Mahamudra Course Texts below).
  • Mahamudra Quote Cards This set of cards contains important quotes, practice tips, and practice reminders from the Mahamudra tradition. It includes tips from some of the greatest masters of the lineage, including Milarepa, Niguma, the Karmapas, and other important teachers. It also includes study flash cards with important Mahamudra frameworks and concepts.
  • The Aspiration Prayer of Mahamudra, the Definitive Meaning, by the 3rd Karmapa, Rangjung Dorje
  • Clarifying the Natural State by Dakpo Tashi Namgyal
  • Crystal Clear by Khenchen Thrangu Rinpoche
  • Moonbeams of Mahamudra by Dakpo Tashi Namgyal, translated by Elizabeth M. Callahan
  • Essentials of Mahamudra: Looking Directly at the Mind by Khenchen Thrangu Rinpoche
